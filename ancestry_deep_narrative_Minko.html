The EDAR Variant — rs3827760 AA

This is the most striking finding in your ancestry profile, and it demands careful interpretation. You are homozygous AA at rs3827760 in the EDAR (Ectodysplasin A Receptor) gene — meaning you carry two copies of the East Asian-derived allele at one of the most famous and studied loci in physical anthropology.

To understand why this is remarkable: the EDAR 370A variant emerged in East Asia roughly 30,000 years ago and swept to near-fixation across East Asian populations. It changes hair follicle thickness (making East Asian hair coarser and rounder in cross-section), sweat gland density, and even breast tissue development and incisor shape. In European populations, this variant is found at frequencies approaching zero — typically less than 1%.

There are three possible explanations for your AA genotype, in order of likelihood:

1. Ancient Steppe-mediated introgression. The Yamnaya pastoralists who contributed 50% of your genome had connections to Central Asian populations via the Afanasievo culture. Some Bronze Age steppe groups carried low levels of East Asian-related ancestry that has been documented in ancient DNA from the Pontic-Caspian region. Your K36 profile shows small but non-zero East Central Asian (0.18%), Amerindian (0.16%), and Indo-Chinese (1.46%) components — these outliers, which most Balkan profiles do not show, may reflect the same ancient Central Asian signal that carried EDAR into your lineage via the steppe populations.

2. Medieval Turkic connection. The Bulgar Khanate, which gave Bulgaria its name, was founded by Turkic-speaking groups from the Pontic steppe in the 7th century AD. These populations had themselves absorbed Central Asian and Siberian genetic material over centuries of steppe movements. A small Bulgar genetic contribution — undetectable in most modern tests but preserved at specific high-frequency variants like EDAR — could explain a homozygous East Asian marker in a Balkan genome.

3. Genotyping or chip artefact. Less likely given MyHeritage's array quality, but worth noting: no ancestry analysis is immune to technical artefacts at individual SNP positions.

The most scientifically defensible interpretation is that your EDAR AA genotype reflects a genuine but small ancient Central Asian or Turkic genetic contribution, carried to the Balkans via the steppe migration routes that account for 48% of your genome. It is the most ancient layer of an extraordinary journey encoded in a single letter change in your DNA.
